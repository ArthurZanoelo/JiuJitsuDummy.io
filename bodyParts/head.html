Rear-Naked Choke

Beginner ▼

The rear naked choke is applied from behind your opponent. Wrap one arm around their neck, with your elbow under their chin. Lock your hand behind your other arm's bicep, then place your free hand behind their head. Squeeze both arms, bringing in your elbows together. One of the easiest and most effective submissions in all of jiu-jitsu.

Triangle Choke

Beginner ▼

The triangle choke is a versatile strangle that involves trapping your opponent's neck and one arm between your legs. To apply it, pull their head down, wrap one leg around their neck, and lock it in with your other leg. Squeeze your knees and pull down on their head to cut off blood flow and force the tap.

Guillotine Choke

Beginner ▼

The guillotine choke is applied from the front, targeting the opponent's neck. Wrap one arm around their neck, with their head under your armpit. Grip your choking hand with your free hand and apply upward pressure while pulling their head down.

Ezequiel Choke

Beginner ▼

The Ezequiel choke is applied from the top position, often inside the opponent's guard. Slip one hand behind their head and grab your own sleeve. Use your free hand to slide across their throat, then apply pressure by pulling your arms in opposite directions. Can be done easily, but does not have a high success rate.

North-South Choke

Intermediate ▼

The north-south choke is applied from the north-south position, with you above your opponent's head. Wrap one arm around their neck while keeping your chest close to the back of their head. Tighten the choke by squeezing your arm and chest together while driving your weight down. Keep in mind, the finish can be quite tricky to get.

Anaconda/Darce Choke

Intermediate ▼

The Darce choke is applied from a top or side position, targeting the opponent's neck and one arm. Thread your arm under their neck and through the armpit, then grab your opposite bicep. Lock your other hand behind their back and squeeze, compressing their neck and shoulder. Mechanics are tricky and can be hard to apply with shorter limbs.

Arm Triangle Choke

Expert ▼

The arm triangle choke involves trapping your opponent's neck and arm. From a top position, push their arm across their neck and wrap your arm around their head. Lock your hands together, lower your head to the mat, and apply pressure by squeezing your arms and driving your weight into their neck. Finishing mechanics are very specific, it can be hard to get the tap without pure strength.
