Straight Ankle Lock

Beginner ▼

This is a basic leg lock where you isolate the opponent's ankle, using a grip around the Achilles tendon and pressing their foot against your ribcage. You then extend your body to apply pressure on the foot, causing pain in the ankle and Achilles tendon. It's one of the most common and legal leg locks across belt levels.

Toe Hold

Beginner ▼

The toe hold involves gripping the toes and twisting the foot downward while controlling the opponent's leg, creating pressure on the ankle, foot, and knee. It's a rotational submission that can target multiple parts of the leg, depending on the setup and leverage used. This technique is often allowed only for higher belt levels due to the potential for injury.

Kneebar

Intermediate ▼

In a kneebar, you isolate the opponent's leg and extend it straight, placing pressure on the knee joint in a hyperextension motion. It's similar to an armbar but applied to the leg, often causing submission due to discomfort or risk of injury to the knee. Kneebars are legal in both gi and no-gi grappling, though they are usually restricted at lower belt levels.

Calf Slicer (Calf Crusher)

Intermediate ▼

A calf slicer compresses the opponent's calf muscle against their shin bone, causing pain and potentially damaging muscle tissue. This is achieved by placing the opponent's calf across your shin and pulling their leg back. The calf slicer is often restricted to advanced practitioners because it can cause muscle and ligament damage.

Heel Hook

Expert ▼

The heel hook targets the ligaments of the knee by twisting the foot outward while holding the heel, putting intense rotational pressure on the knee joint. It is highly effective but can be dangerous due to the risk of severe knee injury. This submission is often only allowed at advanced levels or in no-gi competitions.
