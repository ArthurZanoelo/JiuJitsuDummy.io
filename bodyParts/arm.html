Arm Submissions

Armbar

Beginner ▼

A fundamental submission where the opponent's arm is fully extended, using the hips to apply pressure on the elbow joint. It's commonly applied from mount, guard, or side control. One of the most effective submissions.

Kimura

Beginner ▼

A shoulder lock involving a figure-four grip on the opponent's arm, forcing their hand behind their back and placing immense pressure on the shoulder. It's effective from top and bottom positions. Effective for strong people.

Americana

Beginner ▼

A shoulder lock applied from side control or mount by isolating the opponent's arm and twisting it upward, causing strain on the shoulder joint. Also effective for strong people.

Straight Arm Lock

Intermediate ▼

The straight arm lock hyperextends the opponent's arm by locking it in a straight position and applying pressure at the elbow. Unlike the traditional armbar, which isolates the arm with the legs, this submission can be applied from various positions such as mount or side control, where the arm is straightened and pushed in the direction of hyperextension.

Wrist Lock

Intermediate ▼

Wrist locks target the small and delicate wrist joint by bending or twisting it in an unnatural direction. They can be applied from a variety of positions, including from guard, mount, or even standing. While wrist locks are often subtle and not always prioritized in competition, they are very effective and can be a quick way to catch an opponent off guard, leading to a fast submission. Seen as a cheap submission, it can be really hard to lock the wrist in place.

Bicep Slicer

Expert ▼

The bicep slicer is a compression lock that doesn't directly target a joint but rather the bicep muscle itself. The opponent's arm is trapped between the attacker's forearm and leg, creating an excruciating pain as the muscle is compressed. Though it's primarily a pain submission, it can also cause significant muscle and nerve damage if not released quickly. Effective when used in combination with an armbar.

Omoplata

Expert ▼

A submission where the attacker uses their legs to entangle the opponent's arm, forcing their shoulder to rotate inward. Typically set up from guard, the attacker swings their leg over the opponent's trapped arm and applies pressure by sitting up, targeting the shoulder joint. Besides being a submission, the omoplata can be used to sweep the opponent and gain a dominant position. Difficult to finish in no-gi.
